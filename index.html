What Awaits You

Experiences that stir the soul

From scaling ancient rock fortresses to swimming with blue whales — Sri Lanka delivers once-in-a-lifetime encounters at every turn.

Heritage
Sigiriya Rock Fortress

Climb the 5th-century rock fortress soaring 200m above the plains. UNESCO World Heritage Site adorned with ancient frescoes and royal gardens.

Coastal
Sun-Kissed Beaches

Over 350 beaches line Sri Lanka's coast. From the wild surf of Arugam Bay to the tranquil turquoise of Unawatuna — every mood has its shore.

Wildlife
Safari & Wild Elephants

Yala National Park holds the world's highest density of wild leopards. Spot herds of elephants, sloth bears, and crocodiles in their natural habitat.

Hill Country
Tea Trails & Mist

Wander through emerald tea estates in Nuwara Eliya and Ella. Ride the iconic Kandy–Ella train through some of Asia's most scenic mountain passes.

Culture
Temples & Ceremonies

Visit the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic in Kandy. Witness Esala Perahera — 10 nights of torchlit elephants, drummers, and ancient ritual.

Ocean
Whale Watching & Diving

Mirissa offers some of the world's best blue whale sightings. Pigeon Island and Bar Reef rank among the finest dive sites in the Indian Ocean.

History
Galle Fort

Walk the ramparts of Galle Fort — a Dutch colonial masterpiece. Explore cobblestone lanes filled with galleries, cafés, and living history.

Adventure
Hiking & Nine Arches

Trek to Little Adam's Peak, Ella Rock, or Horton Plains' World's End. The iconic Nine Arch Bridge framed by jungle is one of Sri Lanka's most photographed views.

Wellness
Ayurveda & Wellness

Immerse in authentic Ayurvedic retreats — a 3,000-year healing tradition using herbal treatments, oils, and meditation in lush jungle settings.

The island's iconic destinations

Whether you chase ancient ruins, jungle wildlife, mountain trains, or golden beaches — Sri Lanka's destinations deliver in every season.

North Central Province
The iconic Lion Rock fortress. A 5th-century citadel rising from the jungle with royal gardens, mirror walls, and celestial frescoes.

Living Traditions

A culture alive with ritual

Two and a half millennia of Buddhism, Hinduism, colonial history, and ancient Vedda traditions make Sri Lanka one of Asia's most culturally rich destinations.

Sacred Buddhism

The Temple of the Tooth

The Sri Dalada Maligawa in Kandy houses Sri Lanka's most sacred relic — a tooth of the Buddha. It draws thousands of pilgrims daily and stands as the spiritual heart of the island.

  • Built in the 16th century by King Vimaladharmasuriya
  • Puja ceremonies held three times daily
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1988
  • Centrepiece of the annual Esala Perahera
Grand Festivals

Esala Perahera

Held each July or August, the Esala Perahera in Kandy is the grandest procession in all of Asia. For 10 consecutive nights, decorated elephants, fire dancers, whip crackers, and drummers parade through the ancient streets.

  • Over 100 adorned elephants in procession
  • Thousands of performers and dancers
  • 10 nights of torchlit spectacle
  • One of Asia's oldest living festivals
Ancient Civilisations

The Sacred Triangle

Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa, and Sigiriya form Sri Lanka's Sacred Triangle — three ancient capitals with towering dagobas, carved moonstones, and colossal Buddha statues spanning 2,300 years of civilisation.

  • Anuradhapura — Sri Lanka's first great capital
  • Polonnaruwa — medieval capital with vast reservoirs
  • Sigiriya — the 5th-century cloud fortress
  • All three are UNESCO World Heritage Sites
Everyday Traditions

Food, Art & Craft

Sri Lankan culture lives in its food — rice and curry feasts served on banana leaves — and its crafts: hand-batik textiles, lacquerwork, and the ancient art of mask carving from the Ambalangoda coast.

  • Sinhala & Tamil New Year celebrations in April
  • Kandyan dance — an ancient classical performance art
  • Devil dancing and exorcism rituals (Tovil)
  • Ayurvedic traditions dating back 3,000 years

Travel Essentials

What you need to know

Sri Lanka is one of the world's easiest tropical destinations to navigate. Here's everything to get you started.

🛂

Visa & Entry

Most nationalities require an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) obtained online before arrival.

  • ETA valid for 30 days, extendable to 6 months
  • Apply at eta.gov.lk — takes under 10 minutes
  • Cost: USD 35 (tourist), USD 40 (transit)
  • Citizens of Singapore & Maldives: visa-free
✈️

Getting There

Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB) in Colombo is the main entry point, served by major global carriers.

  • Direct flights from Dubai, Singapore, Doha, London
  • Mattala Rajapaksa Airport (HRI) serves the south
  • Colombo port receives cruise ships year-round
  • Taxi or bus from CMB to Colombo: ~45 minutes
🚂

Getting Around

Sri Lanka has a beloved and remarkably scenic rail network. Combine with tuk-tuks for the full experience.

  • Kandy–Ella train: Asia's most scenic railway route
  • Tuk-tuks for short hops and city navigation
  • Private drivers for flexible multi-day touring
  • Intercity buses: cheap and frequent
💰

Money & Budget

Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). ATMs widely available. Sri Lanka suits all budgets remarkably well.

  • Budget: USD 30–50/day (guesthouses, local food)
  • Mid-range: USD 80–150/day (boutique stays)
  • Luxury: USD 300+/day (jungle lodges, villas)
  • Credit cards accepted at hotels and restaurants
🏥

Health & Safety

Sri Lanka is a safe, welcoming destination. Standard travel precautions apply.

  • No mandatory vaccinations; Hep A & typhoid recommended
  • Tap water: drink bottled or filtered water
  • Sun protection essential year-round
  • Comprehensive private hospitals in Colombo and Kandy
📱

Connectivity & SIM

Local SIM cards are inexpensive and offer excellent 4G coverage across most of the island.

  • Buy SIM at the airport on arrival (Dialog or Mobitel)
  • 1GB data packages from LKR 100 (~USD 0.30)
  • Power: 230V, Type D/G sockets
  • WhatsApp and Google Maps work reliably

When to Go

Sri Lanka is a year-round destination

Two monsoon seasons mean different coasts shine at different times — so there's always a perfect part of the island to visit, whatever the month.

December — March
West & South Coast
Dry and sunny. Best for Colombo, Galle, Mirissa, Bentota, and Negombo. Peak whale-watching season at Mirissa (blue whales from Dec–April). Busiest and priciest period.
Year-Round
Hill Country
Ella, Nuwara Eliya, and Horton Plains are cool and green all year. Light mists are part of the charm. Best experienced January–March or August–September for clearest skies.
May — September
East Coast
While the west coast has its monsoon, the east shines. Trincomalee and Arugam Bay offer calm, crystal waters and world-class surf. Arugam Bay surf season peaks July–September.
July — August
Festival Season
The Kandy Esala Perahera lights up the hill capital. Coincides with the east coast's best weather — combine Kandy's ceremony with Trincomalee's beaches for an unforgettable Sri Lanka summer.
